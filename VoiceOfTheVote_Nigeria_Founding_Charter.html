Section 01
Who We Are
Mission

VoiceOfTheVote Nigeria (VVN) exists to strengthen Nigeria's democratic process through transparent, non-partisan civic education, voter mobilization, and independent election observation — in full alignment with the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC).

Vision

A Nigeria where every eligible citizen is registered, informed, and empowered to exercise their vote in free, fair, and credible elections.

Core Values
⚖
Impartiality
No affiliation with any political party, candidate, or political interest.
◎
Transparency
All activities are open, documented, and publicly accountable.
◈
Non-Partisanship
We serve only the integrity of the process — never any outcome.
◆
Professionalism
All observers are trained, vetted, and certified before deployment.
◑
Inclusivity
Representing women, youth, persons with disabilities, and minorities.

Critical Timeline Markers
Notice of Election

INEC formally issued the Notice of Election on February 13, 2026, marking the official commencement of the 2027 electoral process under the Electoral Act 2026, which requires at least 300 days' notice.

Campaign Periods

Presidential & National Assembly campaigns commence August 19, 2026. Governorship & State Houses of Assembly campaigns commence September 9, 2026. All campaigns end 24 hours before Election Day.

Continuous Voter Registration (CVR)

Phase 1 registered 2,782,589 eligible voters. Phase 2 runs January 5 – April 17, 2026. The full CVR exercise concludes August 30, 2026 — this is the campaign registration deadline for VVN's "Register Now" initiative.

Voter Register Integrity Challenges
  • Duplicate registrations
  • Underage and non-citizen registrations
  • Inclusion of deceased persons
  • Incomplete or inaccurate voter records

"No electoral process can command public confidence without trust in the integrity of its voters' register." — INEC Chairman

Technology Deployed: BVAS + IReV

The Commission is deploying the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) and strengthening the INEC Result Viewing (IReV) portal to enhance transparency. VVN will educate voters on BVAS usage to prevent disenfranchisement.

Section 04
INEC Observer Accreditation Roadmap

This is the most critical pathway for VVN. Seven sequential steps must be completed in order. Begin Step 1 today.

1
CAC Legal Registration
Register VoiceOfTheVote Nigeria as an NGO/CSO with the Corporate Affairs Commission. Obtain Certificate of Incorporation. File your Constitution/Memorandum & Articles of Association, clearly stating a non-partisan, civic/electoral observation mandate.
Mandatory first step
2
FIRS Tax Certificate
Register with the Federal Inland Revenue Service (Medium Tax Office) and obtain the NGO Tax Exemption Certificate. Open a dedicated organizational bank account.
Required for application
3
Register with INEC Civil Society Desk
Visit INEC HQ: Plot 436, Zambezi Crescent, Maitama, FCT, Abuja. Proceed to the Elections and Party Monitoring Department. Obtain your CSO registration number — required before observer applications.
Must obtain CSO number first
4
Complete Form EC 14A (I) — Observer Application
This platform is for organizations only, not individual observers. Obtain and read the published INEC advert before proceeding at www.inecnigeria.org. Prepare CAC registration, CSO registration, past accreditation letters, and reports in PDF format for upload.
Via observergroups.inecnigeria.org
5
Await Approval & Publication
Successful observer groups are published in National Dailies and on the INEC website at the close of the application period. Monitor inecnigeria.org daily via the designated INEC Liaison Officer.
6
Complete Form EC 14A (II) — Deployment Form
Submit the Observer Deployment Form online. Passport photographs (JPEG format) of all deployed members must be arranged on an Area Council basis and submitted with this form.
Online submission
7
Physical Accreditation Tags
Fill accreditation form INEC/EPMC 02 at the Elections and Party Monitoring Department, INEC Headquarters, Maitama. Submit with passport-sized photographs. INEC will produce official identification/accreditation tags for all deployed observers.
In-person · Maitama, Abuja
Section 05
Observer Code of Conduct

Every VVN observer must adhere to the principles below, as mandated by INEC and guided by UN, AU, and ECOWAS treaties and protocols. Any group found to compromise these principles may be banned from future observation.

Permitted
Watch distribution of election materials
Observe voting, sorting, and counting of ballots
Be present at announcement of results
Submit independent observation reports
Prohibited
Handle election materials or ballots
Interfere with polling or counting
Wear any badge or symbol of a political party
Refuse a lawful INEC directive to leave a station
Core Principles

Impartiality · Objectivity · Non-Partisanship — as contained in the INEC Guidelines on Election Observation (2008).

Zero Tolerance

Any falsification of documents in submissions leads to automatic disqualification and possible prosecution. VVN's Legal Officer must double-check all filings before submission.

Section 09
Compliance & Risk Management
⏱
Late CAC Registration
Begin immediately — CAC processing takes 4–8 weeks. File before any other step. Missing this delays all subsequent accreditation milestones.
⚠
Missing Observer Application Window
Appoint a dedicated INEC Liaison Officer to monitor inecnigeria.org daily. Application windows open and close without extensive notice.
◐
Observer Misconduct in the Field
Enforce VVN Code of Conduct strictly. Any observer found in violation is removed immediately. Misconduct can result in VVN being banned from future elections.
◻
Document Falsification
All submissions must be double-checked by the Legal & Compliance Officer before filing. Falsification leads to automatic disqualification and possible prosecution.
◈
Political Infiltration
Implement a strict, documented vetting process for all volunteer observers. Reject applicants with active party affiliations. Repeat screening before each deployment.
